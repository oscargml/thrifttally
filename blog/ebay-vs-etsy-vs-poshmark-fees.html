eBay vs Etsy vs Poshmark: Which One Actually Pays You More?

Updated July 2026 · 6 min read

Sellers usually guess at this based on reputation — "eBay's cheap," "Poshmark takes a lot" — rather than the actual math. The real answer depends more on price point than most people expect.

Each platform structures its cut differently, which means the "cheapest" one changes depending on how much your item sells for. Here's what a seller actually nets on a $20, a $50, and a $150 sale, after every fee.

A few things jump out once the numbers are side by side:

Etsy beats eBay in raw dollars — even though its fees look more complicated

Etsy stacks a listing fee, a transaction fee, and a payment processing fee, which reads as "more fees" than eBay's single final value fee. But added up, Etsy's total (roughly 9.5% of the sale price plus a small flat amount) comes in lower than eBay's roughly 16% combined rate at every price point tested here. The perception that eBay is the low-fee option doesn't hold once you actually add up the line items.

Poshmark's flat 20% is the most expensive of the three above $15

Poshmark's simplicity is also its weakness for anything mid-to-high value: a flat 20% commission means a $150 jacket costs you $30 in fees, more than either eBay or Etsy would take. Where Poshmark holds its own is right around the $15 threshold, where its flat $2.95 fee is genuinely competitive against percentage-based platforms.

Depop's 0% commission shift changed the math entirely

Depop's move to 0% seller commission for US and UK sellers (payment processing only, no separate cut) makes it the highest-net option in this table at every price point — a meaningful shift from its old 10% structure. The trade-off isn't in the fees, it's in the audience: Depop skews toward trend and streetwear buyers, so a formal vintage piece or a rare collectible may simply sell slower there regardless of the better payout.

So which one should you actually use?

Fees are only half the decision — the other half is whether the platform's buyers actually want what you're selling. A lower fee doesn't help if the item sits unsold for six months. As a rule of thumb:

  • Optimizing for net dollars on a piece that could sell anywhere: Etsy or Depop (region-dependent) usually win.
  • Optimizing for the largest possible buyer pool, especially for collectibles: eBay's auction format can outperform a fixed price on the "winning" platforms above.
  • Selling designer or recognizable brand names to an engaged resale community: Poshmark's higher fee can still be worth it for the visibility and repeat buyers.

Every number above assumes standard categories and no store subscription discounts, which can shift the real rate. Run your own item through the calculator to see where it actually lands.
